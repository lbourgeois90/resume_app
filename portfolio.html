Activators/ Do Nows

What are Activators/Do Nows?

It is transition time and students are entering into the classroom. They continue to talk and meander around the room, essentially waiting for the teacher to call their attention to a specific task. Instead of waiting for a teacher to "tell" the students what they "should" be doing, it is a best practice to have a routine in place so students "know" what is expected upon entrance into the classroom. Activators and Do Nows are a great way to ensure this. For example, the routine may be for students to enter the room, sit in their assigned seats, take our their notebook and a pen/pencil... but then what? If given free time the students will just begin to socialize again. Do Nows or Activators are used to probe student thinking, whether it be a question that delves into prior knowledge or asks a student to recap the previous days lesson. Do Nows/Activators can be completed individually, can ask students to reflect and then discuss with a partner or can be a great way to ask an open-ended question that will then be discussed in group or the whole class.
